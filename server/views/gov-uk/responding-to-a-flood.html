Responding to a flood

What to do if you get a flood alert

A flood alert means you need to prepare: flooding is possible

What you should do:

  • keep track of the weather and water levels
  • get ready to act on your flood plan if you have one
  • move vehicles to higher ground if it’s safe to do so
  • prepare a flood kit of essential items including a torch with spare batteries, mobile phone and charger, warm clothes, home insurance documents, water, food, first aid kit and any prescription medicines or baby care items you need
  • check you know how to turn off your gas, electricity and water mains supplies
  • in rural locations, farmers should consider moving livestock and equipment away from areas likely to flood
  • avoid walking, cycling or driving through flood water - 6 inches (15cm) of fast-flowing water can sweep an adult off their feet, while 12in (30cm) can move a car
  • wash your hands if you’ve been in contact with flood water which may contain raw sewage

Businesses

Keep your staff and customers informed.

What to do if you get a flood warning

A flood warning means you need to act now: flooding is expected

What you should do:

  • act on your flood plan if you have one
  • move family and pets to safety
  • move vehicles to higher ground if it’s safe to do so
  • use flood protection equipment like flood barriers and air brick covers
  • move important items upstairs or to a safe place in your property, starting with cherished items and valuables, then furniture and furnishings
  • prepare a flood kit of essential items including a torch with spare batteries, mobile phone and charger, warm clothes, home insurance documents, water, food, first aid kit and any prescription medicines or baby care items you need
  • turn off gas, electricity and water supplies before flood water gets into the property
  • never touch an electrical switch if you’re standing in water
  • if it’s safe to do so, alert neighbours and offer help
  • listen to the emergency services and be ready to evacuate; most flood centres will let you bring your pets
  • avoid walking, cycling or driving through flood water – 6 inches of fast-flowing water can sweep an adult off their feet, while 12 can move a car
  • wash your hands if you’ve been in contact with flood water which may contain raw sewage

Businesses

  • act on your business flood plan if you have one
  • move your staff and customers to a safe place
  • move stock and other valuable items upstairs or to a safe place in your building

What to do if you get a severe flood warning

A severe flood warning means lives are at risk: you must act now

What you should do:

  • call 999 if you’re in immediate danger
  • evacuate your property if you’re told to do so; most evacuation centres will let you bring your pets
  • act on your flood plan if you have one
  • move your family and pets to safety
  • use flood protection equipment (such as flood barriers, air brick covers and pumps) to protect your property
  • move important items upstairs or to a safe place in your property starting with cherished items and valuables, then furniture and furnishings
  • pack a bag for a few nights away in case you need to move out
  • make sure you have an emergency kit including a torch with spare batteries, mobile phone and charger, warm clothes, home insurance information, water, food, first aid kit, prescription medicines and, if needed, baby care items
  • turn off your gas, electricity and water mains supplies before flood water gets into your property
  • never touch an electrical switch if you’re standing in water
  • if it’s safe to do so, alert neighbours and offer help
  • avoid walking, cycling or driving through flood water – 6 inches of fast-flowing water can sweep an adult off their feet, while 12 can move a car
  • wash your hands if you’ve been in contact with flood water which may contain raw sewage

Businesses

  • act on your business flood plan if you have one
  • move your staff and customers to a safe place
  • move stock and other valuable items upstairs or to a safe place in your building
